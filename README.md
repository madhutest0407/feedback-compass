# VoxPulse — Feel the pulse of your market

> Search what customers are really saying — across Reddit, Twitter, G2, Capterra,
> and Trustpilot — in one place, ranked by what matters most.

---

## The Problem

Customer feedback is everywhere — buried in Reddit threads, scattered across
review sites, hiding in tweets. By the time you've read through all of it,
you've spent hours just finding the signal.

**VoxPulse changes that.**

Type a product name, a topic, or a feature. In seconds, VoxPulse pulls real
feedback from across the web, sorts it by severity, and gives you a sentiment
score — so you always know where to focus first.

---

## Who Is This For?

- **Product Managers** who need to understand customer pain before roadmap planning
- **Marketers** tracking how a campaign, product launch, or competitor is landing
- **Researchers** who want unbiased, real-world market sentiment at scale

---

## What You Get

### 🎯 One Search, Five Sources
Reddit threads, X/Twitter posts, G2 reviews, Capterra ratings, and Trustpilot
feedback — all in a single, unified feed. No switching tabs.

### ⚡ Severity-First Results
Not just a list — a prioritised one. Critical issues surface at the top.
Positive highlights at the bottom. You see what needs attention first.

### 📊 Sentiment Scorecard
A 0–100 score that tells you at a glance whether the market feels good, neutral,
or at risk. Watch it trend over time as you refresh.

### 💾 Save & Track
Save any search as a view. Refresh it next week, next month. See how sentiment
shifts after a product update, a pricing change, or a competitor's launch.

---

## Example Searches

- `"Notion onboarding"` — what new users really struggle with
- `"Figma performance"` — how designers feel about speed
- `"Linear pricing"` — is the market reacting to recent changes?
- `"Slack notifications"` — what's the biggest pain point?

---

## Getting Started

1. Clone this repo
2. Add your API keys (Firecrawl + Lovable AI) to `.env`
3. Run `bun install && bun run dev`
4. Open `localhost:3000` and start searching
